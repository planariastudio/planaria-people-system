1 Output Quality & Craft
Pacing & rhythm
  • On a supervised slice, pacing follows the reference and is fixed with direction
  • Pacing feels intentional on standard projects; minor notes only
  • Cuts feel intentional; pacing matches content tone and client style
  • Pacing holds on complex multi-thread edits with no direction
  • Sets the pacing bar for the team; catches drop-off in others' cuts
  • Defines the studio pacing reference others are measured against
Storytelling structure
  • Assembles a clear sequence from a provided outline
  • Clear arc on standard formats; holds without re-cut
  • Clear narrative arc; talking-head well-shaped from raw footage
  • Shapes structure on complex stories end to end, no structural intervention
  • Owns the story standard across the team; fixes others' structure
  • Sets the structural approach the hardest projects are built on
Cut precision
  • No sync errors after a supervised check
  • No jump cuts, pops, or sync errors on standard deliverables
  • No jump cuts, audio pops, or sync errors in final deliverable
  • Frame-clean on complex timelines; zero technical cut errors
  • Upholds a zero-defect cut standard across the team
  • Reference for cut precision; defines the QC bar for cuts
Brand & style adherence
  • Follows the style guide on a supervised slice
  • Matches one client's identity reliably
  • Output matches client visual identity across all accounts
  • Holds brand across many accounts and formats without slips
  • Owns brand consistency across the team's output
  • Sets reusable brand/style systems the team adopts
First-cut approval rate
  • Not measured; works on supervised slices
  • At least 45% of first cuts need only minor revisions
  • At least 65% of first cuts need only minor revisions
  • At least 80% of first cuts need only minor revisions
  • At least 85%, and raises the team's first-cut rate
  • At least 85% on the hardest briefs
2 Client & Revision Management
Average revision rounds
  • 3 total rounds or fewer, at most 1 structural, on a supervised slice
  • 3 total rounds or fewer, at most 1 structural
  • 3 total rounds or fewer, at most 1 structural, across all clients
  • 2 total rounds or fewer, no structural re-cut
  • 2 or fewer total, no structural; team average trending down
  • 2 or fewer total, no structural, on the hardest projects
Revision cause breakdown
  • With help, can say why a revision happened
  • Identifies revision cause on own projects
  • Names per project whether a revision came from a brief misread, client change, or craft issue
  • Predicts likely revision causes and prevents them
  • Tracks revision causes across the team and fixes root causes
  • Designs the practices that remove common revision causes
Brief adherence
  • Follows a checklist under supervision; no missed items after review
  • No missed requirements on standard briefs; checklist used
  • No missed requirements from the brief; checklist used before submission
  • No missed requirements on complex, multi-part briefs
  • Owns the brief-intake checklist standard for the team
  • Sets the brief-intake standard the team works to
Client feedback interpretation
  • Applies clearly-stated feedback correctly
  • Handles direct feedback without clarification
  • Translates subjective feedback into edit decisions without supervisor clarification
  • Reads ambiguous or conflicting feedback and resolves it
  • Coaches the team on reading feedback; handles escalations
  • The person tricky feedback is escalated to on craft grounds
3 Speed & Turnaround
On-time delivery rate
  • 90% of supervised task deadlines met
  • 85% of internal deadlines met
  • At least 85% of internal deadlines met across all projects
  • At least 90% met, including on complex projects
  • At least 90% personally, and the team hits its deadlines
  • At least 90% on the hardest projects
Turnaround vs. estimate
  • Works to a supervisor-set estimate
  • Actual hours within 30% of the estimate
  • Actual edit hours within 20% of the scoped estimate per project
  • Within 15% on complex projects
  • Within 15%, and scopes estimates for others
  • Within 15% on the hardest work; sets estimation benchmarks
Throughput
  • Completes assigned supervised slices on schedule
  • Completes expected volume for a single-client load
  • Completed the expected volume of deliverables for the quarter
  • Carries a heavier or more complex load at volume
  • Balances the team's load to hit collective throughput
  • Sustained top-tier output on the hardest projects
Proactive deadline flagging
  • Raises a slipping task as soon as noticed
  • Flags at-risk deadlines at least 48h ahead
  • Any at-risk deadline flagged at least 48 hours ahead, no surprise misses
  • Flags risks 48h+ ahead across several projects
  • Flags team-level risk 72h+ ahead and reallocates
  • Anticipates risk on the hardest projects before it lands
4 Technical Skills (Premiere Pro)

  • Owns the colour/finishing standard for the team
