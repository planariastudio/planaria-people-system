How the three tools work, and how to fill them in

Three instruments, one rating scale. This page walks through what each one is for and the exact steps to complete it — skim the section for whichever one you've been asked to open.

Rating scale
1 to 5, used everywhere
1–2 low 3 fair 4–5 high
Pass bar
3.0 (Fair) on KPI and PIP.
Peer Appraisal has no pass bar — it's developmental, not a score you clear.
Career ladder
Intern → Jr → Associate (Video Editor) → Senior → Supervisor or Principal (parallel tracks, same weight)

KPI Scorecard

quarterly review · editor + supervisor

If you're the editor

You rate yourself first. Once you lock it, your side is final — supervisors take over from there.

  1. Open the form and choose I'm the editor.
  2. Pick your name, then the quarter — a Q1–Q4 dropdown plus the year, not typed freely.
  3. Work through each category: rate yourself 1–5 and add a short note with real examples for each metric.
  4. On the last step, name your own growth focus for next quarter and answer the written questions.
  5. Lock your scores. This can't be undone from your side — it hands off to your supervisor(s).
  6. Copy the case link shown after locking and send it to whoever is supervising you.

If you're the supervisor

You score independently — you won't see other supervisors' scores until you file.

  1. Choose I'm a supervisor and enter the supervisor password when asked (ask your lead if you don't have it).
  2. Pick the editor and quarter, or open the case link they sent you directly.
  3. Their case only loads once they've locked their side — if it hasn't, you'll see a note telling you to wait.
  4. Rate each metric yourself; the editor's self-score shows alongside as a read-only reference, not something you edit.
  5. Add your written assessment, then submit your scores. Up to three supervisors can score the same case.
  6. Any one supervisor can then file to ClickUp — the official score is the mean of every supervisor who submitted.
Note — the supervisor stage is password-gated so an editor can't land there by accident and see the scoring screen meant for someone else.

Peer Appraisal

anonymous · rated by colleagues who work with you often

Getting your link

You don't sign up — you're assigned. A supervisor picks who rates whom each cycle and sends you a personal link.

One link, forever. The same link works for every peer you're ever asked to rate — you don't need a new one each cycle.

Filling it in

  1. Open your personal link and pick who you're rating, plus the cycle (Q1–Q4 + year).
  2. For each value, compare where they are now against the next level anchors shown, then rate 1–5.
  3. Write one key strength and one thing to grow — specific and observed, not general.
  4. Rating a senior? You'll also be asked which way they seem to be growing: IC track, Management track, or too early to say.
  5. Review your ratings, confirm the anonymity statement, and submit.
Your rating is anonymous. The pooled report for that person stays hidden until at least two peers have rated them in the same cycle — so a single rating can never be traced back to you.

PIP

Performance Improvement Plan · filled with the editor, not about them alone

What it is

A single working document a manager and editor build together when someone needs formal, time-boxed support to hit the bar — not a one-time form, a live case you keep coming back to.

Filling it in

  1. Pick the editor, the severity, and a start date — duration and review date fill in automatically from those.
  2. Gaps & targets: list each competency that's below bar, with evidence, a measurable target, and what support the company provides.
  3. Actions: what the editor will do and what the company provides, each with a date.
  4. Checkpoints: dated check-ins fill in automatically from the start date — log on-track status and notes as you go.
  5. Outcome: once scores are in, the verdict (Pass / Not Met) calculates itself — pick the matching result and write the summary.
  6. Save progress any time — it's the same case link every time you come back. File verdict only once you've reached a final call; this closes the case for good.
If the auto-verdict and your chosen result don't match (e.g. verdict says Pass but you pick Exit), the form will stop and ask you to confirm before filing — it never resolves the conflict silently.
A few things people ask
What if I click into the wrong side by mistake?

On the KPI form, if an editor ends up on the supervisor screen, there's a link back to self-review right there — no need to close and reopen the page.

What does a case ID like KPI-2026-Q3-Fitra-Pratama-01 mean?

Instrument, year, quarter, the editor's first and last name, then a running number for that quarter. Same pattern for PIP. You'll see it on the form once you've picked an editor and quarter — it's how you and your supervisor find the same case again.

I saved progress on a PIP or supervisor score — how do I get back to it?

Every save shows a case link. Bookmark it or keep it in the message thread — opening that link loads everything you'd already entered.

Where do I check the full rubric wording?

Every form links to "View full rubric" — the same reference table for all six levels, kept in sync with what the forms actually use.
